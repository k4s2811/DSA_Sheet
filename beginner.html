LeetCode and Codeforces aren't quite the same kind of practice. LeetCode covers interview-style DSA, including data-structure-design problems that Codeforces doesn't really have. Codeforces is organized by tag + numeric rating (800 = easiest, 3500+ = hardest) rather than fixed lists — so on days without a small set of verified classic problems, you'll see the exact tag and rating range to filter by on codeforces.com/problemset instead.
CF rating ↔ LeetCode difficulty, roughly: 800–1200 ≈ Easy · 1300–1700 ≈ Medium · 1800+ ≈ Hard-and-up. Since you already know the concepts, start most new CF topics around 1200–1400 rather than 800 — those actually require the technique instead of just brute force.
